About

Building human capability through community integration.

The Signals Institute transforms underserved communities and addresses critical workforce gaps in advanced technology and engineering through early youth exposure, structured veteran transition pathways, and open-access community engagement. Our purpose is simple: make technical opportunity visible, reachable, and durable.

Mission

Expand access to advanced technology careers

From first exposure to job placement, we build structured pathways that meet people where they are.

Where

Riviera Beach, Florida

Community-first programs delivered through schools, libraries, and local partners.

Related

Anchored by research + philanthropy

Related organizations include Anne Huntington Laboratory (research institution) and Anne Huntington Foundation (supporting foundation).

The Challenge

Talent exists. Access does not.

Communities are full of capable people. The barrier is exposure, mentorship, and clear pathways into research and engineering work. Veterans bring rare national capital—technical foundations, leadership experience, and mission discipline—yet often lack civilian on-ramps that preserve momentum.

01

Youth without access

Young people rarely encounter technical careers early enough to see themselves in them.

02

Veterans left behind

Transition systems underutilize technical MOS experience and specialized capability.

03

Disconnected ecosystems

Defense research and national security capability often operate in isolation from the communities that can sustain them.

Pillars

A continuum of deepening involvement.

Each pillar represents a depth of participation—not a separate program. Engagement builds the relationship. Exposure changes what feels possible. Transition builds toward durable outcomes. Youth, veterans, and community members each move through engagement, exposure, and transition at their own pace.
01

Engagement

Youth · Veterans · Community

Engagement is the front door. The Institute comes to you. No technical content, no prerequisites, no commitment—just relationship, awareness, and trust. The goal: you know we exist, and you feel welcome.

  • Monthly Researcher Forums
  • School & community outreach
  • Veteran orientation & peer networking
02

Exposure

Youth · Veterans · Community

Exposure is the experience. You come to the Institute. Hands-on, guided discovery—you are actually touching equipment, seeing research, trying things. You have seen what is possible and felt it yourself.

  • Summer Discovery Camp
  • Researcher Workshop Series
  • Hands-on demonstrations & lab tours
03

Transition

Youth · Veterans · Community

Transition is where the Institute builds durable capability. Structured, outcomes-focused work: career development, skill-building, internship pipelines, and employer placement.

  • DOD SkillBridge partnership
  • Employer pipeline & direct placement
  • After-school & summer intensives

How it works

A repeatable cadence that builds trust.

We design engagement as an operating system: consistent events, predictable pathways, and measurable follow-through. Each event points participants to the next step—youth programs, veteran transition, or continued community learning.

01

Partners provide space (library, school, city facility). We bring facilitators and curriculum.

02

Deliver

Workshops are hands-on and simple: radios, signals, software tools, and real career narratives.

03

Convert to pathways

Participants exit with a clear next step: program track, intake, mentorship, or partner referral.
