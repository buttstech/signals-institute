Youth Exposure
#youth
#ages-10-14

Young people are the long-term foundation of technical workforce capacity. Early exposure creates cultural familiarity where research and technical work become natural extensions of community capability. By demystifying scientific reasoning and advanced technology, we transform these fields from something distant and mysterious into something attainable and expected.

We recruit researchers and develop suitable presentation agendas on their behalf, bringing working scientists directly into schools and community spaces. The goal is inspiration—making advanced technical work visible to young people who might otherwise never encounter it.

The Institute handles all logistics; researchers only need to show up and engage. Activity-driven sessions are brought directly to schools and community spaces, where students experience the concept before they learn its name.

Our Programs
#workshops
#summer-camp
#hands-on

Researcher Workshop Series: Monthly school-year sessions bringing working scientists to youth, reaching 20 participants per session across Palm Beach County schools.

Summer Discovery Camp: A 2-week intensive experience with rotating researchers, serving 40 youth each summer with hands-on activities in RF, signals, and emerging technology.

Activity Library: We maintain a living curriculum of hands-on activities designed to surface concepts through doing rather than instruction. A student builds a wire antenna, watches it pick up a signal, and asks why—the concept emerges from experience.
