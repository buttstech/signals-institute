Youth Programs
#exposure
#workshops
#stem

We serve youth ages 10-18 through two tracks. For younger students, we bring working researchers directly into schools and community spaces—activities first, concepts second. For high school students, we offer structured career development with project-based programs, internship placements, and publication opportunities through the Signals Youth Research Journal.

Track A — Exposure (Ages 10-14):

• Researchers from any domain recruited and paired with hands-on activities — Institute handles all logistics
• Monthly researcher workshops during the school year
• Two-week Summer Discovery Camp with rotating researchers
• Activities are the vehicle; concepts emerge through doing

Track B — Career Development (Ages 14-18):

• Semester after-school technical program — project-based, facilitator-led
• Summer Technical Intensive — 6 weeks, students ship something real
• Science Fair Mentorship — year-round, one-on-one with researchers
• Internship placements with defense and technology employer partners

Veteran Programs
#skillbridge
#clearance
#defense-employment

Military personnel transitioning to civilian careers represent the highest-leverage population for building national technical capability. Veterans bring security clearances, technical foundations, disciplined work habits, and mission orientation.

Custom Pathways — Not Fixed Programs:

• Intake assessment drives an individualized pathway for every veteran
• Content areas: professional transition, RF & defense technology, AI & software tools
• All instruction facilitator-led by industry professionals, retired military, and faculty
• DOD SkillBridge Industry Partner — eligible for active-duty participation
